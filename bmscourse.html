BMS Courses

BMS stands for Bachelor of Management Studies. Bachelor of Management Studies is an undergraduate job-oriented management course. The course duration is 3 years. The eligibility criteria to pursue the BMS course is, students should complete the 12th standard. BMS course consists of six semesters, in which each academic year consists of 2 semesters. This course focuses to educate individuals regarding ethics and the development of management. This is a good scope for BMS graduates since it deals directly with the growth of management. This course also helps to improve qualities like - leadership and Entrepreneurship.

Eligibility

The eligibility criteria for a BMS course specify that:
  • Candidates must have passed 10+2 with at least 50- 60 per cent marks from a recognized board.
  • Candidates should have passed 10+2 from any stream like Arts, Science or Commerce.
  • The students are generally admitted on the basis of personal interview and written examination conducted by various institutes/universities.
  • Some institutes/universities admit student on merit basis.

Admission

The admissions to B.M.S course is done on the basis of marks secured by the candidates in 10+2 or equivalent examination and entrance test conducted by different institutes. Most of the colleges conduct their own entrance test to shortlist the candidates for the course. The entrance exam mainly has questions from English and Communication Skills, General Knowledge, Creative Thinking, Logical Reasoning, Data Interpretation and Economics. Many colleges also conduct personal interview or Group discussion followed by the entrance test to shortlist the candidates. Candidates who qualify all the rounds involved in the admission process of an institute, get admission to the Institute.

Career & Jobs

As the media is a flourishing sector, it has various types of career opportunities for the candidates. As the sector is considered a pillar of the society, B.M.M. graduates can get different types of job roles. Candidates can be Journalist, content writer, editor and much more. Bachelor of Mass Media opens various areas for the graduates. Those with a B.M.M. Degree can get following job roles-
Journalist - The job role of a journalist is to collect news or information from various sources and present before the public. The role also involves working on articles and stories. The average annual salary of a journalist in India is 3.50 lacs.
Columnist - A columnist is a person that writes or edits copies for a regular column in a newspaper. This column can be related to any field- entertainment, politics, sports, fashion and many more. The average annual salary of a columnist in India is 3.60 lacs.
News Reporter - The job role of a news reporter involves gathering and writing news, reporting on-air, conducting interviews, generating stories and more. The average annual salary of a news reporter in India is 2 lacs.
Editor - The job role of an editor involves planning, coordinating and revising the content for publication. The main work is to check the copy of content that is going to be uploaded. Other than this, their job role involves setting publication standards, suggesting stories, complying with the media laws and more. The average annual salary of an editor in India is 4 lacs.

Salary

The salary of B.M.M. graduates range from 2 lacs to 7 lacs per year. A fresher with a B.M.M. degree may get an average annual salary package of 2 -2.4 lacs but the salary is increased with the experience. With an experience of five-six years, a graduate can earn 6-7 lacs per annum. The salary is further increased with the years.
